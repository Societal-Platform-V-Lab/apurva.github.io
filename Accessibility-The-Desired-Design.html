Accessibility: The Desired Design

Accessibility is the protagonist who has the ability to break down barriers across various avenues like resources, opportunities and information. And most importantly, it stands out as the desired design for any route.

Accessibility offers equal opportunities for inclusive participation and engagement, regardless of individual differences or the circumstances. It aims to create an inclusive and equitable space where everyone can participate and contribute. As listed above, there are various barriers to accessibility. These can be categorised into four main types: physical, digital, information, and social. 


Accessibility is built on the concept of inclusion and equity, where the focus is not only about equal opportunities and access, but also on meeting the different requirements of different people. Implementing accessibility relies on identifying and responding to the ability of people from different walks of life to access needs, opportunities and goals across various circumstances. The equitable solutions for accessibility designed have to balance inclusion of population and at the same time uphold privacy, independence and integrity for everyone.

Research says that the most suitable solution would be to identify the problem in terms of accessibility and remove it rather than provide means to overcome the problem. This is called the liberation stage. For instance, with growing digital platforms and increasing use of technology opens up the world for people with disabilities. Capabilities like translating text to speech, speech to text and even simple video calls have made it possible to break barriers of communication. This reminds me of my friend’s excitement to witness a couple who were deaf having a quick chat on video call with sign language!

Let us take a look at digital accessibility during the pandemic. Since the world was locked down and there was a sudden jump to digital platforms for various things, it negatively affected the people who lacked digital skills and digital infrastructure resulting in digital vulnerability. Thus, many were isolated and cut off from the world, losing opportunities and spaces of employment, health, information and networking. Reimagine if the design had considered the large population of digitally illiterate people, especially for booking slots for vaccines, with spaces where people who don’t have access can be helped by others. Or multiple options could have been offered for vaccine slots.

The desired design

It is a welcoming space for all which changes the so-called normal and also creates new standards of inclusion. The combination of accessibility and inclusive space translates into values, processes and training, finally encouraging embracing differences in people. Accessibility leads to:

  • More independence and participation 
  • Diversity of perspectives 
  • Social cohesion and harmony
  • Resilience and adaptability of society

Working towards accessibility, one has to steer towards empowering people by designing for diversity across populations, age and culture. A multi-dimensional approach and involving different actors helps in designing for accessibility, where the solutions can be customised and localised easily across infrastructures, working processes and norms. Just like how technology is helping to bridge communication routes amongst people with disabilities, people from different linguistic backgrounds, where it can be customised, localised and translated into different preferred mediums.

Big picture for accessibility


The workings of Apurva.ai, founded on collective wisdom, aims to offer an inclusive accessible space. Rachna KG, Growth Lead, says that one of the bare minimum requirements for inclusiveness is accessibility. She elaborates that considering the diverse needs of the population, Apurva is designed to be accessible across different languages, various modes (like text, audio and videos) and multiple channels (like web, whatsapp, interactive voice response (IVR) and call centres). 

Incorporating as part of the default design, accessibility, as the protagonist, opens doors to access information, activities and environments in a sensible, meaningful and usable way by a diverse range of people. This, we believe, opens up the world to more people, knitting a tight, inclusive space for all to share, participate and grow in. What is your route for accessibility as the desired design?
